Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU)

Sun Yat-sen University (SYSU)

Sun Yat-sen University, founded by Dr. Sun Yat-sen in 1924 and with an educational tradition spanning over 100 years, is a preeminent research, academic and cultural center and the premier location for talent development.

Under the direct supervision of the Ministry of Education of the People’s Republic of China, and strongly supported by both the Ministry and Guangdong Province, Sun Yat-sen University has developed into a modern comprehensive university that enjoys a reputation as a top-tier university nationally and a renowned university internationally. With five campuses in the three cities of Guangzhou, Zhuhai and Shenzhen, and eleven affiliated hospitals, the University is striving to become a world-class university and global center of learning.

Built on a solid multidisciplinary foundation of humanities, social sciences, natural sciences, medical sciences, and engineering, Sun Yat-sen University is propelled forward by the continuous pursuit of academic innovation. The University has dedicated itself to being an institution that is “comprehensive, innovative, and open”. Now, standing at a new starting point, Sun Yat-sen University strives to enter both the national first-tier of universities and the ranks of world-class elite universities, building a first-class socialist university with Chinese characteristics.

Research

As a comprehensive research university, the drive toward innovation motivates the entire campus community. SYSU'S funding for scientific research of over 3 Billion RMB (2020 annual incoming) and the large number of projects supported are indicators of the university's rapid development. The universities ranks 1st among Chinese universities for national social science funded projects and 2nd for both national natural science funded projects and MOE humanities and social science funded projects.

The university has 28 national research platforms, including four state key laboratories in oncology, ophthalmology, optoelectronic materials, biocontrol, and 199 provincial and ministerial research platforms.

Engagement with IU

Indiana University's partnership with Sun Yat-sen University dates back to 2007 when IUPUI initiated an alliance with SYSU.

  • From 2014 through the start of the pandemic, the Schools of Nursing at both universities had hosted small numbers of students, led by their respective nursing faculty, for experiential learning based on observation. Since 2020, the schools have continued to collaborate remotely via virtual connections.
  • In 2018, IU and SYSU signed a sub-agreement that represented their shared interest in cooperating on aspects of the TianQin project to advance their mutual objectives in research and education.
  • In 2019, former IUPUI Chancellor Nasser Paydar visited SYSU and reconfirmed IU's continued interest in the partnership.
